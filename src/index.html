Marcus Aurelius Quotes

Marcus Aurelius was a Roman emperor who ruled from 161 to 180 CE. He was born in Rome in 121 CE to a prominent family, and was adopted by the reigning emperor Antoninus Pius, who became his father and mentor. Marcus Aurelius was trained in philosophy and rhetoric from a young age, and he developed a strong interest in Stoicism, a philosophy that emphasizes self-control, rationality, and virtue.

As emperor, Marcus Aurelius was faced with numerous challenges, including wars with the Parthians and Germanic tribes, economic problems, and political corruption. Despite these difficulties, he is remembered as one of the most admirable rulers in Roman history, in part because of his personal qualities and commitment to Stoicism.

Marcus Aurelius is best known for his Meditations, collection of personal writings that Marcus Aurelius composed as a private diary during his campaigns in the East. It offers a glimpse into his personal struggles, his beliefs about human nature, and his duty as an emperor. The diary reflects his deep empathy for his fellow human beings, and his conviction in the power of reason and self-control to surmount adversity.
